ABOUT ME

I am a software engineer in the Greater Boston Area specializing in educational technology with a background in product management and market research.

A musician and a bookworm, I graduated from Harvard in 2010 with a joint degree in English and Music and entered the world of publishing as a market researcher/data analyst at Scholastic. After conducting research for the launch of Storia (an e-reading app), curiosity about the software development process led me to transition into a Product Management role.

The experience of successfully deploying several education apps internationally and overseeing two distributed teams of engineers drove my desire to build applications myself. I started teaching myself Python in my free time, discovered I loved it, and took the leap to attend Hackbright Academy* and devote myself to programming full-time.

I love solving problems and building things with code more than I've loved anything else, and I am constantly working to learn new technologies and grow my engineering skills.

I am particularly fascinated by the parallels between programming and music composition and enjoy writing beautifully composed code.

* Hackbright Academy is an intensive software engineering fellowship for women in San Francisco with an acceptance rate of 7.5%. Hackbright Fellows learn Python, JavaScript, HTML/CSS, SQL/ORMs, Flask, Git, CLI, data structures and algorithms, and pair programming. Graduates now work at companies such as Facebook, Pinterest, Uber, Stubhub, Survey Monkey, and Eventbrite.
